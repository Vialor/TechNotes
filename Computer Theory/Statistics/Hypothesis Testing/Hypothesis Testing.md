**Alternative hypothesis**: hypothesis to prove
**Null hypothesis**: hypothesis to disprove
**p-value**: probability of obtaining the observed results, assuming that the **null hypothesis** is true
a result has **statistical significance** when a result at least as "extreme" would be very infrequent if the null hypothesis were true.
**Central limit theorem**: the sampling distribution of the mean will always be normally distributed, as long as the sample size is large enough
## Tests
**Z-Test**
assess whether a sample mean is significantly different from a known or hypothesized population mean
$z={\mu_1-\mu_2 \over SEM}$﻿
1. It relies on population parameters
2. It relies on central limit theorem - it doesn't work for small samples
**T-Test**
compare the means of two groups and determine if there is a statistically significant difference between them
$t={\bar{x_1}-\bar{x}_2 \over SEM}$﻿
